Payetools Licensing Model

Payetools is released as open-source software under the GNU Affero General Public License (AGPL). This license requires developers wishing to integrate Paytools components within a larger system to publish the source code of that system under an AGPL-compatible license.

For most commercial organisations, however, the demands of AGPL are too restrictive. For this reason, Payetools is also offered under a commercial licensing mode, details of which are available upon request.

Frequently asked questions

  • Is Payetools really open-source, given it is also available under a commercial license?

    Yes, the AGPL is recognised as an Approved License by the Open Source Initiative (see https://opensource.org/license/agpl-v3/). Paytools Foundation offers an alternative (commercial) license for organisations that do not wish to be bound by the terms of the AGPL.

  • Why did adopt the GNU Affero General Public License, and not a more permissive license such as MIT?

    Much has been written about sustainable open-source - the concept that an open source project needs a way to gain funding in order to ensure its long-term viability. Paytools' adoption of AGPL, alongside a concise yet entrepreneur-friendly commercial license ensures a modest revenue stream to support the ongoing maintenance and enhancement of the software and oversight of the project.

    (It is unfortunately not legally possible to dual-license a piece of software combining a permissive license such as MIT with a commercial license, as the permissive license always "trumps" the commercial license.)

  • What about example code, is that covered by AGPL?

    No, all example code, both within any Payetools package, and within the Payetools online documentation is released under the MIT No Attribution License, meaning it may be used without restriction, and without any need to attribute its source. (See https://opensource.org/license/mit-0/).
